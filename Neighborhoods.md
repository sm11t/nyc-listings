# Neighborhood Comparison

> **Status:** Research complete (2026-04-25). Each tier table below feeds into [[Schedule-vs-Housing]]. Top picks have deep-dive notes in `housing/neighborhoods/`.

## Constants

- **Campus:** NYU Tandon, 6 MetroTech Center, Brooklyn (Jay St-MetroTech subway, A/C/F/R)
- **Budget ceiling:** **$1200/mo all-in** (room in shared apt, utilities included if possible)
- **Profile:** F1 international student (India), no US credit history, no car, MTA + bike
- **Commute tolerance:** 30-90 min one-way *only if on campus ≤2 days/week*

## Hard market reality (April 2026)

- NYC vacancy rate: **~1.4%** (historically tight)
- Median Bushwick rent: **$3,400** for whole apt; **rooms-in-share are clustering at $1,150–$1,650**
- **$1,200 ceiling is achievable but not the median** — requires aggressive shopping in Tier B/C/D
- **FARE Act (June 2025) is in force** as of April 2026 pending Second Circuit ruling — landlord broker fees no longer pass to tenant. Filter "no broker fee" on RentHop/StreetEasy.

## NYC market notes (April 2026)

### Best month to start
For Aug 2026 move: **start mid-June, sign by mid-July.** Listings volume triples Jan→July (~27,300 new in July alone). The proven NYU pattern: (a) lock 30-day August sublet by late June, (b) arrive early August, (c) sign year-lease in person by mid-August.

### FARE Act status
- Took effect **June 11, 2025**. DCWP has issued ~50 summonses against ~1,400 complaints.
- REBNY appeal at Second Circuit; oral arguments November 2025; **no ruling as of late April 2026** — law still in force.
- **Practical:** filter "no broker fee" on RentHop/StreetEasy. If broker tries to charge tenant-side fee on landlord-listed apartment, that's now illegal — report to DCWP.

### F1 guarantor reality
- **Insurent / TheGuarantors:** 70-110% of one month's rent (one-time fee) for non-US-credit renters. ~$840-$1320 upfront on a $1200 room.
- **Cheapest workaround:** **2-6 months prepaid rent** to a small landlord. F1 students with sufficient I-20 funds proof commonly take this route.
- **Second cheapest:** **take over a roommate's spot in an existing share.** Bypasses guarantors entirely — only need landlord's verbal OK + your portion of security to existing tenants.

1. **[[Sunset-Park]]** — best price-to-commute ratio. R direct, sub-$1100 rooms, Chinese intl-student infra.
2. **[[Crown-Heights]]** — best Reddit consensus. 2/3/4/5 + Franklin shuttle, $1100-1200 rooms, transplant-friendly.
3. **[[Journal-Square]]** — F1 dark horse. Indian/South Asian community, sub-$1150 rooms, only viable if ≤2 days/wk on campus.
4. **[[Bed-Stuy]]** — large brownstone share inventory, 18-25 min A/C, $1100-1300 sweet spot.
5. **[[Bushwick]]** — densest sublet market in NYC; pricier ($1175-1300) but easiest to find a room mid-summer.
