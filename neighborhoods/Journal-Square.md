# Journal Square, Jersey City

> **Top pick (5 of 5).** F1 dark horse — strongest cultural + cost fit for an Indian international student. Only viable if Fall 2026 schedule confirms ≤2 days/week on Tandon campus.

## Why consider it

**Largest concentration of Indian + South Asian international students in the metro after Jackson Heights.** Sulekha + WhatsApp roommate networks are dense and active. Sub-$1150 private rooms are realistic. PATH (Journal Sq → WTC, 12 min) → 2/3 or walk Fulton + R = 35-45 min total to Jay St.

## Rent reality

- **Low:** $810 (older walk-up, room-share, far from PATH)
- **Median:** $1,150 (private room, 5-min walk to PATH)
- **High:** $1,525 (newer building, en-suite)

What $1,200 actually gets you: a private room in a 2-3BR apt with desi roommates, walking distance to PATH, often utilities-included. Furnished options common via Sulekha. New construction (148-unit luxury announced for 2026 at 413 Summit) adds inventory.

## Commute

- **PATH Journal Sq → WTC:** 12 min direct.
- **WTC → Jay St-MetroTech:** 2 or 3 train, 8-12 min (one stop after Wall St).
- **Total:** 35-45 min door-to-door. Add 5 min if you walk from WTC to the R at Cortlandt St.
- **PATH cost:** $3.00 (matches MTA fare).
- **Frequency:** PATH runs every 4-8 min off-peak; 24/7 service; reliable.
- **The friction:** crossing the Hudson is psychologically and logistically more than crossing within Brooklyn. **Forget late-night spontaneous Manhattan plans.**

## Vibe & life

- **South Asian heart of NJ:** dosa restaurants, Indian groceries (Patel Brothers, India Bazaar), Bangladeshi cafes, mandir.
- **Best Indian food outside Jackson Heights** — and cheaper.
- **Newport Mall:** 10-min walk; full mall with Target, AMC, Whole Foods.
- **Improving fast** but 5+ blocks east of PATH still gets dicey at night — stick to the immediate JSQ blocks.
- **Loyola Mountainside Hospital, NJCU, Saint Peter's University** all in the area = student-adjacent feel.
- **No real nightlife/scene** — Manhattan is the destination.

## F1 notes

- **F1-friendly is very high:** landlords are used to no-credit Indian students. Many rentals advertise "Indian roommates only" or "vegetarian household."
- **Sulekha Indian Roommates** = primary marketplace.
- **WhatsApp groups** through NJIT / Stevens / NYIT Indian student associations leak into JSQ housing.
- **2-3 months prepaid rent** is standard; many landlords skip guarantor requirements entirely if you offer 4 months upfront.
- **Strong word-of-mouth network** — once you land one apartment in JSQ, you'll hear about every opening within months.

## Verdict

**Best for:** the student who (a) has confirmed ≤2 days/week on Tandon campus, (b) wants Indian community + food + cultural ease, (c) values lowest cost-per-month over commute time.

**Avoid if:** schedule is 3+ days/week (commute hours kill you), or if a primary goal is the Brooklyn AI-creative-grad-student scene (you'll feel cut off).

**My take:** **Decide between Journal Square and Sunset Park last** — they're both ~$1100 rooms but optimize for different things. JSQ wins on community + food + room size; Sunset Park wins on commute (20 min vs 40 min) and on access to the Brooklyn ecosystem. **Schedule lock determines this.** If the Fall 2026 slate clusters on Mon+Wed evenings only, JSQ is rational. If it's Mon+Tue+Thu, Sunset Park.
