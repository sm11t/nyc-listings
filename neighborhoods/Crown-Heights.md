# Crown Heights, Brooklyn

> **Top pick (2 of 5).** Best value-to-commute ratio in Brooklyn proper. The "default Reddit answer" when grad students ask where to live for Tandon on a budget.

## Why consider it

The 2/3/4/5 from Franklin/Nostrand/Kingston-Throop runs to Atlantic Av-Barclays (~12 min) where you transfer to R for one stop to Jay St — total 15-22 min door-to-door. Rooms cluster $1,100-1,200 right in the budget center. SpareRoom shows ~353 share rooms here at any given time.

## Rent reality

- **Low:** $900 (smaller back room, 4BR walk-up, far from train)
- **Median:** $1,200 (private room, 5-min walk to subway, utilities split)
- **High:** $1,450 (renovated brownstone, large room)

What $1,200 actually gets you: a private room in a 3BR brownstone or walk-up apt, 5-10 min from Franklin/Nostrand line, often utilities-split. Slightly tighter inventory than Bed-Stuy but better commute.

## Commute

- **2/3 from Franklin Av:** Atlantic Av (12 min) → R one stop to Jay St-MetroTech = ~17 min total.
- **4/5 from Franklin Av:** Same Atlantic Av transfer = ~15 min.
- **Walk to Jay St from station:** 3 min.
- Late-night: 4/5 doesn't run nights; rely on 2/3. Adds 5-10 min of headway.
- **Bonus:** Franklin Av Shuttle (S) connects to Prospect Park and the Q.

## Vibe & life

- **Franklin Avenue corridor:** restaurants, coffee shops (Little Skips, Blank Slate), cocktail bars. The most "transplant-y" stretch.
- **Nostrand Avenue:** Caribbean restaurants (Trinidadian doubles, Jamaican jerk, $8-12), traditional Lubavitch Jewish blocks south of Eastern Pkwy.
- **West Indian Day Parade (Labor Day):** the neighborhood is *very* loud for that weekend; otherwise residential.
- **Lincoln Market** on Franklin = organic + standard. Multiple bodegas + C-Town for bulk.
- **Prospect Park** is a 10-min walk south.

## F1 notes

- Stable, lots of long-term shares advertising on **Gypsy Housing FB group** + SpareRoom.
- Many small landlords (1-4 unit brownstones); accept guarantor services or **2-3 months prepaid rent**.
- Reddit r/AskNYC: west of Nostrand is consistently rated low-anxiety for solo male grad students; east of Utica gets a different review.

## Verdict

**Best for:** the "balanced" answer. You get a real Brooklyn neighborhood with food and bars, a 17-min commute, and budget headroom. The "no regret" pick.

**Avoid if:** you want a Manhattan-feeling vibe (it's residential), or if you're sensitive to nightlife-noise spillover on Franklin (especially weekends).

**My take:** **The default if I can't be in Sunset Park.** Strong choice for any class schedule from 2-5 days/week. Eastern edge (toward Utica) is cheaper but commute creeps to 25+ min and walking-home-late comfort drops.
